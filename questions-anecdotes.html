The Anatomy of a Question

Someone asked a question. “Why does an apple drop from a tree and not go upwards?” This singular question resulted in today’s knowledge about gravity. Similarly, questions have been asked and today we can boldly say, The Earth is round and not flat. Questions have also led to the knowledge of present times: Electricity, computers, Travel (jets), food, drinks, games, and lots more. Let’s think for a second, If these questions were never asked, The great limitations the world may have faced. Now also think, if everyone asked a question. How much more advancements we would have. A Question is required to obtain information, To know more, to learn. The quest to understand the world; The desire to relate with certain phenomenon, The intense curiosity of the mind Or the inquisitiveness of humans, Sometimes, simply seeking clarity. These and more drive questions. Questions come in different forms, But reach for the same goal – Enlightenment. We ask because we do not know, We ask because we want to know. We may not ask for the fear of being mocked, Or uncertainty about what questions may be right or wrong. It may be an avoidance of being intimidated, Or simply, the overconfidence that “we know it all”. There is no such thing as a dumb question. What makes a Question intelligent or stupid? The person who’s asking? The content of the Question? Or the one who’s being asked? The same thing that makes a Question intelligent Is also what makes it stupid. You ask because you do not know And what I know is not what you know. Hence, what may seem a stupid question to me, May just be intelligent to you. So what then? Ask! You want to get better? You need to understand? That’s the beauty of a Question. Better to look stupid for 5 minutes Than remain stupid for a lifetime.
